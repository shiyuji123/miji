Here are fundamental physics concepts in English with structured explanations:

---

### **1. Mass & Weight**
**Definition**:  
- **Mass**: Measure of an object's matter content (scalar quantity)  
- **Weight**: Force exerted by gravity on mass (vector quantity)  

**Formula**:  
\[ W = m \times g \]  
*(W: weight, m: mass, g: gravitational acceleration ≈ 9.8 m/s²)*  

**Units**:  
- Mass: kilogram (kg)  
- Weight: newton (N)  

**Example**:  
A 60kg person on Earth:  
Weight = 60 kg × 9.8 m/s² = 588 N  

---

### **2. Newton's Laws of Motion**
**First Law (Inertia)**:  
"An object maintains its state unless acted upon by external force."  

**Second Law**:  
\[ F = m \times a \]  
*(F: net force, m: mass, a: acceleration)*  

**Third Law**:  
"For every action, there's an equal and opposite reaction."  

**Application**:  
Car airbags demonstrate First Law by resisting sudden deceleration.  

---

### **3. Energy Forms**
| Type          | Description                  | Formula           | Unit   |
|---------------|------------------------------|-------------------|--------|
| Kinetic       | Motion energy                | \( KE = \frac{1}{2}mv^2 \) | joule (J) |
| Potential     | Position/stored energy       | \( PE = mgh \)    | joule (J) |
| Thermal       | Heat energy                  | \( Q = mc\Delta T \) | joule (J) |

**Conservation**:  
Energy cannot be created/destroyed, only converted (e.g., pendulum motion).  

---

### **4. Electromagnetism Basics**
**Charge Types**:  
- **Proton**: +1.6×10⁻¹⁹ C  
- **Electron**: -1.6×10⁻¹⁹ C  

**Ohm's Law**:  
\[ V = I \times R \]  
*(V: voltage, I: current, R: resistance)*  

**Circuit Components**:  
- **Conductor**: Allows electron flow (e.g., copper)  
- **Insulator**: Blocks electron flow (e.g., rubber)  
- **Semiconductor**: Controlled conductivity (e.g., silicon)  

---

### **5. Wave Properties**
**Key Parameters**:  
- **Wavelength (λ)**: Distance between crests  
- **Frequency (f)**: Oscillations per second  
- **Speed (v)**: \( v = f \times λ \)  

**Spectrum Types**:  
| Type          | Frequency Range      | Application        |
|---------------|----------------------|--------------------|
| Radio         | 3 kHz - 300 GHz      | Broadcasting       |
| Visible Light | 400-790 THz          | Human vision       |
| X-ray         | 30 PHz - 30 EHz      | Medical imaging    |

---

### **6. Thermodynamics Laws**
1. **Zeroth Law**: Thermal equilibrium definition  
2. **First Law**: \( \Delta U = Q - W \) (Internal energy change)  
3. **Second Law**: Entropy always increases  
4. **Third Law**: Absolute zero unattainable  

**Practical Example**:  
Refrigerators demonstrate 2nd Law by requiring energy to transfer heat.
